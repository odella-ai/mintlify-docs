---
title: "Terminate"
description: "Remove a worker from your workforce and clean up connected channels and triggers."
---

**Terminate** removes the worker from your workforce.

Termination is for workers you no longer need. If you only want to pause work temporarily, use the runtime toggle instead.

## What happens during termination

When you terminate a worker:

- You are asked to confirm the action.
- The worker is removed from your workforce.
- Connected channels and triggers are cleaned up.
- The worker should no longer process normal work.

<Warning>
Termination is destructive. Use it only when you are sure the worker should be removed.
</Warning>

## Pause instead of terminate

Use the runtime toggle when you want to temporarily stop a worker without deleting it.

Pausing is better when:

- You are investigating behaviour
- You are changing settings
- You need a temporary operational hold
- You may need the worker again later
